Project Lead The Way at BHS

Belton High School offers a wide variety of Project Lead The Way classes. These classes focus on biomedical science, computer science, and engineering. These courses are; Principles of Biomedical sciences, Human Body Systems, Medical Intervention, Biomedical Innovation, Computre Science and Software Engineering, Computer Science Applications, Computational Problem Solving, Cybersecurity, Introduction to Engineering Design, Principles of Engineering, Digital Electronics, Computer Integrated Manufacturing, Manufacturing Technician Certificate Program, and finally Engineering Design and Development. Descriptions of each course below.PRINCIPLES OF BIOMEDICAL SCIENCES​(PBS) Course#: FYR1351

Course Descriptions

Grade: 9 Type of Credit:​1 Elective PREREQUISITES: ​B or higher in math and science classes and previous year attendance 90% or higher. COURSE DESCRIPTION:​Recommended for students interested in health science careers. Students investigate the human body systems and various health conditions including heart disease, diabetes, sickle­cell disease, hypercholesterolemia and infectious diseases. They determine the factors that led to the death of a fictional person and investigate lifestyle choices and medical treatments that might have prolonged the person’s life. This course is designed to provide an overview of all the courses in the Biomedical Sciences program and lay the scientific foundation for subsequent courses. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ HUMAN BODY SYSTEMS (HBS) Course # HYR1353 Grade: 10 Type of Credit: 1 Elective Science Credit PREREQUISITES:​B or higher in PBS COURSE DESCRIPTION:​Students examine the interactions of human body systems as they explore identity, power, movement, protection, and homeostasis. Exploring science in action, students build organs and tissues on a skeletal Maniken®; use data acquisition software to monitor body functions such as muscle movement, reflex and voluntary action, and respiration; and take on the roles of biomedical professionals to solve real­world medical cases. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ MEDICAL INTERVENTIONS (MI) Course # HYR1355 Grades: 11­12 Type of Credit: 1 Elective Science Credit PREREQUISITES:​B or higher in HBS COURSE DESCRIPTION:​Students follow the life of a fictitious family as they investigate how to prevent, diagnose, and treat disease. Students explore how to detect and fight infection; screen and evaluate the code in human DNA; evaluate cancer treatment options; and prevail when the organs of the body begin to fail. Through real­world cases, students are exposed to a range of interventions related to immunology, surgery, genetics, pharmacology, medical devices, and diagnostics. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ BIOMEDICAL INNOVATION (BI) Offered: 2017­18 school year Grade: 12 Course # HYR1357 PREREQUISITES:​B or higher in MI COURSE DESCRIPTION:​In the final course of the PLTW Biomedical Science sequence, students build on the knowledge and skills gained from previous courses to design innovative solutions for the most pressing health challenges of the 21st century. Students address topics ranging from public health and biomedical engineering to clinical medicine and physiology. They have the opportunity to work on an independent design project with a mentor or advisor from a university, medical facility, or research institution. COMPUTER SCIENCE & SOFTWARE ENGINEERING (CSE) Grades: 9­12 Course #: FYR1500 (BFC) Course #: HYR1637 (BHS) Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit PREREQUISITES:​Previous programming experience preferred but not required. COURSE DESCRIPTION:​The course is project­ and problem­based, with students working in teams to develop computational thinking and solve open­ended, practical problems that occur in the real world. The course is not a programming language course; it aims instead to develop computational thinking, to generate excitement about the field of computing, and to introduce computational tools that foster creativity. This course aligns with the AP Computer Science Principles course. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ COMPUTER SCIENCE APPLICATIONS (CSA) Grades: 10­12 Will begin​​2017­18 school year Course #: HYR1641 Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in CSE COURSE DESCRIPTION:​CSA focuses on integrating technologies across multiple platforms and networks, including the Internet. Students collaborate to produce programs that integrate mobile devices and leverage those devices for distributed collection and data processing. Students analyze, adapt, and improve each other's programs while working primarily in JavaTM and other industry­standard tools. This course prepares students for the AP Computer Science­A course. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ COMPUTATIONAL PROBLEM SOLVING (CPS) Grades: 11­12 Will begin​​2018­19 school year Course #: HYR1643 Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in CSA COURSE DESCRIPTION:​As a capstone course, CPS offers students the opportunity to work in a team to deliver a software solution to a real­world design problem. Teams start by defining problems then will use the Agile design process to develop a software solution. Effective practices in problem solving, documentation, software development, presentation, and collaboration are central to the course. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ CYBERSECURITY (SEC) Will begin​​2019­20 school year Grades: 11­12 Course #: HSM1638 PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in CSA COURSE DESCRIPTION:​SEC introduces the tools and concepts of cybersecurity and encourages students to create solutions that allow people to share computing resources while protecting privacy. Nationally, computational resources are vulnerable and frequently attacked; in SEC, students solve problems by understanding and closing these vulnerabilities. This course raises students' knowledge of and commitment to ethical computing behavior. Type of Credit: 1/2 Practical Art Credit INTRODUCTION TO ENGINEERING DESIGN (IED) Grades: 9­10 Course #: FYR1629 (BFC) Course #: HYR1629 (BHS) Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit PREREQUISITES:​B or higher in Algebra I COURSE DESCRIPTION:​Students will use 3D design software to help create solutions to solve proposed problems. Students will learn how to document student work and communicate solutions to peers and members of the professional community. The major focus is to expose students to the design process, research and analysis, engineering standards, and technical documentation. Students will use math on a daily basis in this class. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ PRINCIPLES OF ENGINEERING (POE) Course #: HYR1631 Grades: 10­12 Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in Introduction to Engineering Design (IED) COURSE DESCRIPTION:​This course of engineering exposes students to some of the major concepts they’ll encounter in a postsecondary engineering course of study. Students employ engineering and scientific concepts in the solution of engineering design problems. Students also learn how to document their work and communicate their solutions in a professional learning community. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ DIGITAL ELECTRONICS (DE) Course #: HYR1633 Grades: 11­12 Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in POE COURSE DESCRIPTION:​Digital electronics is the foundation of all modern electronic devices such as cellular phones, MP3 players, laptop computers, digital cameras and high­definition televisions. The major focus of the course is to expose students to the process of combinational and sequential logic design, teamwork, communication methods, engineering standards and technical documentation. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ COMPUTER INTEGRATED MANUFACTURING (CIM) Grades: 11­12 Course #: HYR1639 PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in POE COURSE DESCRIPTION:​Manufactured items are part of everyday life, yet most students have not been introduced to the high­tech, innovative nature of modern manufacturing. This course illuminates the opportunities related to understanding manufacturing. At the same time, it teaches students about manufacturing processes, product design, robotics, and automation. Type of Credit: 1 Practical Art Credit 26 MANUFACTURING TECHNICIAN CERTIFICATE PROGRAM (MT1) Grades: 11­12 Course #: HYR1645 PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in POE COURSE DESCRIPTION:​The MT1 training program addresses the core technical competencies required for skilled production occupations in all sectors of manufacturing: math and measurement, business acumen and quality, spacial reasoning and manufacturing technology. Training includes safety, quality practices and measurement, manufacturing processes, electrical and mechanical basics and maintenance awareness. The MT1 Certification is an industry credential that demonstrates to employers anywhere in the world that an individual has an understanding of modern manufacturing. ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­ ENGINEERING DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT (EDD) Grade: 12 Course #: HYR1635 PREREQUISITES:​C or higher in POE COURSE DESCRIPTION:​This is the engineering Project Lead the Way (PLTW) capstone course. It is designed for in­depth study in engineering. Students will research, design, and construct a solution to an open­ended engineering problem. Students will also apply principles developed in preceding courses, present progress reports, submit a final written report and defend their solutions to a panel of outside reviewers at the end of the school year.
